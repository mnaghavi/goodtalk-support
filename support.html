What we can help with

Most support questions fall into one of these areas. If you are not sure where your issue belongs, just email us and include what happened.

Account and sign-in

Phone sign-in, Apple or Google sign-in, profile setup, username questions, and verification issues.

Messaging and AI

Coach Mode, Touch Up, @aria, message refinement, approvals, and AI budget or feature access concerns.

Connections and invites

Phone discovery, contact matching, invite codes, finding someone by @username, or pairing problems.

Premium and billing

Questions about GoodTalk Premium, trials, subscriptions, restore purchases, or access differences between free and Premium features.

Product feedback and bugs

If something feels confusing, broken, or incomplete, send the steps you took and what you expected to happen. Feature suggestions are welcome too.

Helpful details to include

A little context makes support much faster. You do not need a formal report, but these details are useful when something is not working.

For bugs or broken flows

  • What you were trying to do
  • What happened instead
  • Whether the issue is repeatable
  • Your device and iOS version if relevant

For account or billing questions

  • The email or sign-in method tied to the account
  • Whether the issue is about Premium, trial access, or restore purchases
  • Any error messages you saw
  • Whether the problem affects one device or more than one

Quick answers

These are the most common questions implied by the app's current feature set.

What is GoodTalk for?

GoodTalk is a messaging app focused on clarity, care, and confidence in emotionally important conversations. It combines messaging with AI-supported coaching, refinements, and in-chat assistance.

Do I have to share my phone number with everyone?

Not necessarily. The app supports multiple discovery paths, including phone-based discovery, short-lived invite codes, and unique @username handles.

What is the difference between Coach Mode and Touch Up?

Touch Up is lighter and focuses on grammar, clarity, and tone. Coach Mode is more structured and aims to help reshape difficult messages into something more constructive.

What does @aria do?

@aria is an in-chat assistant that can help with wording, advice, perspective, and general conversational support while you are messaging.
