Coach

For the talks you really do not want to mess up.

Coach is for conflict, tension, and emotionally loaded moments. It helps turn reactive drafts into messages that are clearer, steadier, and easier for the other person to hear without losing what you really mean.

  • Built for harder conversations where tone and emotional clarity matter
  • Includes review and approval before a refined message is sent
  • Can show a private coaching insight about why the message changed
  • Premium-only mode for deeper communication support
Where Coach shines Different conversations, same calmer landing.
Relationship

When things have felt off lately

You want to bring up distance without sounding accusing or cold.

Your draft

"You barely make time for us anymore and I'm tired of pretending it's fine."

Coach reframes

"I miss how connected we used to feel, and I want to talk about how we can make more space for each other again."

Why it works: It keeps the emotion, drops the blame, and opens the door to a real conversation.
Work

When feedback needs to land well

You need to be direct with a teammate without making the exchange feel harsh.

Your draft

"You keep missing details and it's slowing everyone down."

Coach reframes

"A few details have been slipping through lately, and I think tightening that up would make the whole project move more smoothly."

Why it works: It stays clear, but makes the feedback easier to hear and respond to.
Teenager

When the goal is to guide, not explode

You want to hold the line without turning the whole conversation into a shutdown.

Your draft

"You never listen and I'm done arguing about this. Just do what I said."

Coach reframes

"I know this has been frustrating for both of us. I'm not trying to fight with you, but this boundary still matters and I want us to talk about it with respect."

Why it works: With teenagers, tone matters as much as the rule. It keeps authority without making the conversation harder to recover from.
Aria

A capable assistant, right inside the chat.

Aria works from the conversation already on screen. Mention @aria and it can read the thread, pick up on what each person said, and answer with context instead of acting like a blank chatbot.

  • Use @aria directly inside a conversation
  • Can react to the thread itself, not just the one line you typed
  • Helpful for advice, quick judgment calls, brainstorming, and everyday questions
  • Supports measurement preferences and optional location-aware context
  • Available on free with limits, with broader access on Premium
How people might use Aria Quick prompts, practical answers, right where the conversation is happening.
Local ideas

Find a spot nearby

You want a quick suggestion without leaving the chat.

@aria prompt

"@aria find a cozy restaurant near me for tonight."

"You could try a small Italian place nearby with quieter seating, or a casual wine bar if you want something more relaxed."
Perspective

Let Aria call it

You want a fun, blunt read based on what both people already said in the thread.

@aria prompt

"@aria who is right?"

"Sarah is more in the right here. You said you only texted after you were already running 40 minutes late, and Sarah said she was left waiting without a real heads-up. Based on that exchange, her frustration makes more sense."
Planning

Figure out the next move

You want ideas that are thoughtful but still easy to act on.

@aria prompt

"@aria give me three easy ways to make tonight feel special at home."

"Cook something simple together, put phones away for an hour, or set up a low-key movie night with a small surprise snack."
Touch-up

For the messages that only need a better finish.

Touch-up is the lighter, faster mode. It keeps your original meaning intact while cleaning up grammar, improving clarity, and softening the edges just enough to help your message land better.

  • Best for everyday texting, quick edits, and better readability
  • Designed to preserve your voice instead of rewriting the whole message
  • Useful when you want polish without a full coaching pass
  • Works naturally with the app's approval flow before a message is sent
  • Included on free with usage limits and expanded on Premium
What Touch-up changes The meaning stays yours. The phrasing just lands better.
Everyday text

Clean up a rushed message

You typed fast, but the intent is already there.

Original

"hey sorry im late traffic is awful can u start without me"

Touch-up

"Hey, sorry I'm late. Traffic is awful. Can you start without me?"

Same meaning, just cleaner and easier to read.
Sharper tone

Soften the edges, not the point

You still want to be clear, just not harsher than you mean to be.

Original

"You said you'd handle this and now I'm stuck dealing with it."

Touch-up

"I thought this was already handled, so I was caught off guard when it came back to me."

The message still says the same thing, but it sounds less combative.
Awkward wording

Keep your voice, lose the mess

You know what you mean, but the wording feels clunky.

Original

"I want hang later if your still around cause I kinda need get out a bit."

Touch-up

"I'd love to hang out later if you're still around. I could use getting out a little."

It still sounds like you, just more natural and finished.

Features

Everything is grouped around better communication, not clutter.
