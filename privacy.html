Privacy

Plain-language privacy for a product built around sensitive conversations.

GoodTalk is built for meaningful conversations, so privacy and user control matter. This page gives a simple overview of how the app uses information to keep the product working well.

Quick summary

GoodTalk uses information like account details, messages, profile settings, optional discovery data, and support communications to operate the app. Some features rely on services for authentication, real-time messaging, push notifications, subscriptions, storage, and server-side AI processing. GoodTalk is built around messaging and support, not ad-driven tracking.

What GoodTalk may use

Information tied to the features people actually use.

Account and profile

GoodTalk may use information such as your name, username, avatar, linked sign-in providers, and basic account identifiers so your profile and conversations work as expected.

Messages and message metadata

Messages, drafts, timestamps, visibility state, and related metadata are used to deliver chats, show message status, and support features like refined message approval and conversation history.

Phone verification and discovery

If you verify a phone number or enable phone-based discovery, GoodTalk may use that information to help people find each other, depending on their settings and yours.

Contacts access

If you allow contacts access, GoodTalk can help identify which people in your contacts are already on the app. The product is designed to use privacy-aware matching rather than exposing raw contact lists in the product experience.

How features use data

Product-accurate explanations of the main flows.

AI processing for Coach, Touch-up, and Aria

GoodTalk uses server-side AI processing to refine messages, generate coaching guidance, and answer requests made through @aria. This lets the app provide those experiences as part of the product rather than treating them like on-device-only features.

Optional location for Aria

If you grant location access, GoodTalk can use location context to improve certain Aria responses, such as local or measurement-aware answers. Location access is optional and tied to that experience.

Notifications and device tokens

If you enable notifications, GoodTalk may use push-notification permission status and device token information so message alerts and related notification features work correctly.

Subscriptions and Premium

GoodTalk uses App Store subscription information to manage Premium access, restore purchases, and distinguish between free and Premium feature availability.

Services and providers

The app depends on outside infrastructure to function.

Authentication providers

GoodTalk supports sign-in through phone, Apple, and Google, depending on the option a person chooses.

Messaging and storage infrastructure

The app uses cloud infrastructure for authentication, messaging, storage, real-time sync, subscriptions, and notification-related functions.

Server-side AI providers

AI-enabled features depend on server-side model providers to process supported requests and return responses for refinement, coaching, and assistant flows.

App-level settings and preferences

GoodTalk stores local preferences such as selected defaults and presentation choices so the app can remember how you prefer to use it.

Your controls

Choices available inside the app today.

Privacy and discovery controls

  • Enable or disable discoverability by phone
  • Use @username so people can find you without a phone number
  • Set last-seen visibility
  • Choose whether to grant contacts or location access

Product and account controls

  • Choose a default style for new chats
  • Adjust notification and sound preferences
  • Choose Aria measurement preferences
  • Manage Premium through the App Store
